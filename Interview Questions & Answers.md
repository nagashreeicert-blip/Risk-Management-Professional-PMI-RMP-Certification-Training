## üü¢ Beginner-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What is Risk in Project Management?**  
An uncertain event that can affect project objectives positively or negatively.

2. **What is Risk Management?**  
The process of identifying, analyzing, and responding to project risks to minimize threats and maximize opportunities.

3. **Who is a Stakeholder?**  
Anyone affected by or who can influence the project outcomes.

4. **What is a Risk Register?**  
A document that lists all identified risks, their assessment, and planned responses.

5. **What is Risk Appetite?**  
The degree of uncertainty an organization or individual is willing to accept in pursuit of objectives.

6. **What is Qualitative Risk Analysis?**  
Assessing the probability and impact of risks using descriptive techniques like risk scoring.

7. **What is Quantitative Risk Analysis?**  
Using numerical methods to calculate potential risk impact, often with Monte Carlo simulations.

8. **What are Risk Response Strategies?**  
Methods to address risk: Avoid, Mitigate, Transfer, Accept.

9. **What is a Contingency Plan?**  
A pre-defined action plan to address identified risks if they occur.

10. **What is a Risk Owner?**  
The person responsible for monitoring and managing a specific risk.

## üü° Intermediate-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What is a Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS)?**  
A hierarchical representation of risks organized by categories or sources.

2. **Explain Risk Probability and Impact Matrix.**  
A tool used to prioritize risks based on their likelihood and potential effect on the project.

3. **What is Expected Monetary Value (EMV)?**  
A quantitative technique to calculate the average impact of risk using probability √ó impact.

4. **What is Monte Carlo Simulation in risk management?**  
A statistical technique to simulate project outcomes by analyzing multiple scenarios.

5. **What is a Risk Trigger?**  
An event or condition indicating a risk is about to occur.

6. **What is Risk Escalation?**  
The process of reporting a risk to higher management when it exceeds a certain threshold.

7. **What is Risk Audit?**  
A systematic review of the risk management process and its effectiveness.

8. **Explain Risk Tolerance.**  
The acceptable level of variation around objectives that stakeholders are willing to accept.

9. **What is Residual Risk?**  
The risk that remains after risk response strategies have been applied.

10. **What is Secondary Risk?**  
A new risk that arises as a direct result of implementing a risk response.

## üî¥ Advanced-Level Questions & Answers (10)

1. **What is Risk Governance?**  
The framework and policies that guide risk management within an organization.

2. **Explain Sensitivity Analysis.**  
A technique to determine which risks have the most impact on project objectives.

3. **What is Risk Interdependency?**  
The situation where one risk affects or triggers another risk.

4. **What is Risk Modeling?**  
Creating simulations or models to predict potential risk outcomes and impacts.

5. **What is a Risk Management Plan?**  
A formal document detailing how risk management will be performed, monitored, and controlled.

6. **How do you monitor and control risks in a project?**  
By tracking identified risks, assessing new risks, executing response plans, and updating the risk register.

7. **Explain Decision Tree Analysis in Risk Management.**  
A visual tool to evaluate risk options and select the best course of action based on probability and impact.

8. **What are Risk Categories?**  
Common types include technical, financial, schedule, operational, external, and regulatory risks.

9. **How do you handle high-impact, low-probability risks?**  
Prepare contingency plans, assign ownership, and monitor closely.

10. **How do you measure Risk Management effectiveness?**  
Through KPIs like number of risks mitigated, % of risks avoided, and accuracy of risk predictions.
